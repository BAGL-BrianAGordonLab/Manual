General Lab Expectations

Time Tracking and Work Hours

As a member of this lab, you have the flexibility to manage your schedule as long as you fulfill your commitments, meet project deadlines, and maintain open lines of communication with your supervisor and team. Staff and postdoctoral associates are expected to work 40 hrs/wk, graduate students: 20 hrs/wk, and undergraduates: 10 hrs/wk. We trust that you will prioritize your responsibilities and ensure your work contributes to the overall success of the lab.

Attendance

As a member of our lab, you are expected to attend seminars, weekly lab meetings, 1-on-1 meetings, start and end of semester events, as well as the annual graduate research symposium. These gatherings provide valuable opportunities for knowledge exchange, scientific discussion, networking, and showcasing your research, contributing to a collaborative atmosphere and enhancing your professional growth within the scientific community.

Communication Channels and Tools

We promote seamless communication through regular team meetings, Microsoft Teams, and email updates. We value open dialogue, active listening, and respect for diverse perspectives; and we encourage transparent and timely communication to ensure everyone is aligned on project goals, milestones, and any potential challenges.

Social Etiquette

We encourage lab members to represent themselves and our research with professionalism and respect when attending conferences or scientific meetings. This includes actively engaging in presentations, asking thoughtful questions, and providing constructive feedback to fellow researchers. We also promote networking and building connections with peers and experts in the field.

Maintaining Health

We understand the significance of maintaining a healthy work-life balance and encourage everyone to prioritize self-care, manage their workload effectively, and set boundaries to ensure a balance between work and personal life. We believe that taking time for oneself, pursuing hobbies, spending quality time with loved ones, and engaging in activities outside of work contribute to overall productivity and satisfaction. By fostering a supportive and flexible environment, we aim to help our lab members achieve a sustainable work-life balance that promotes both personal happiness and professional growth.

Leaving the Lab

When the time comes to leave our lab, we strive to make the transition as smooth as possible. We understand that career paths may diverge, and we support individuals in their decisions to pursue new opportunities. Exiting lab members are encouraged to maintain connections and utilize the resources available for networking and future collaborations. Our goal is to foster a supportive environment even beyond their tenure, promoting growth and success in their future endeavors.

Code of Conduct

We value integrity and uphold ethical standards for the responsible use of resources and data; harassment, discrimination, and any form of misconduct will not be tolerated. We work together as a team, supporting and uplifting one another, and recognizing the contributions of all members.
