Leave Policies and Procedures

Our leave policies provide guidelines for various types of leave, including personal, medical, and family-related reasons. Eligible employees can request leave through a formal process, which involves notifying your supervisor and HR via WorkDay. Leave durations and benefits vary based on the type of leave and individual circumstances.

Benefits

Our comprehensive benefits package encompasses health, dental, and retirement plans, fostering the overall well-being and financial security of faculty and staff, postdoctoral appointees, clinical fellows and trainees, and retirees. You and your family have access to benefits and resources, such as health care coverage, employee wellness program, tuition assistance and help in establishing your financial future.

Travel and Expense Reimbursement

We offer comprehensive travel reimbursement that covers conference registration fees, airfare, ground transportation, hotel lodging, and per diems. Brian must first approve travel before an expense report can be created in WorkDay. Please note that conference registration fees and airfare can generally be reimbursed prior to the conference, while lodging, transportation, and per diems are reimbursed after the event. Keep in mind that daily per diem rates may vary depending on the location (city, state, and country), and the Per Diem Meal and Indicental Form must be completed on submission. Your understanding and adherence to these procedures will help ensure a smooth reimbursement process.

Data Security and Confidentiality

When working with human subjects, maintaining the security of sensitive information is crucial. If you come across any security concerns, potential breaches, or incidents involving sensitive data, it's essential to promptly notify (314-747-2955) the Office of Information Security.

Comprehensive Security for File Sharing
We prioritize the protection of your data when sharing files within and outside our lab. By utilizing secure platforms and encryption methods, we ensure that sensitive information remains confidential and safeguarded throughout the sharing process. Microsoft Outlook, Microsoft Teams, Microsoft Yammer, and Box are the only approved communications software for sensitive data. Under NO circumstances can anything data or patient related be communicated via Meta, Twitter, Messenger, Instagram, Snapchat, or TikTok.

Intellectual Property and Authorship Guidelines

As lab members, you will have the opportunity to contribute to the development and writing of manuscripts, ensuring that your intellectual contributions are recognized and acknowledged. Authorship is determined based on the established criteria of substantial intellectual contribution and active participation in the research process. We adhere to ethical standards and guidelines for authorship, promoting fairness, transparency, and accountability.

Our lab provides essential office supplies and resources to support your work, ensuring you have the tools needed for efficient research and productivity. XNAT: Open Source Imaging Informatics Platform
The function of an XNAT is to import data from scanners, archive or save data into the database, securely share imaging and related study data, and data processing capabilities via Docker containers.
In our lab, we use the CNDA for Benzinger ADRC and other data, FORNIX for Benzinger and Goyal Lab projects (Racial Disparities and ADOPIC), DCA for all DIAN study data, and TIP for Barnes clinical processing.

Employee Services

Identification Cards
For reasons relating to institutional identity, the promotion of safety for all members of the WUSM community and to obtain certain special services and privileges, WUSM Protective Services issues identification badges to all faculty, staff and students as well as selected contract employees. Your immediate supervisor can identify the individual with access to submit your ID badge request in ServiceNow, and badges can be picked up at the Facilities Integrated Service Center (FISC).

The badge entitles employees to a number of benefits and privileges, including but not limited to:
• Access to the medical school and Danforth Campus libraries
• Access to the Athletic Complex at the Danforth Campus
• Access to the faculty/staff dining plan
• Tax-free purchases via BearBucks at cafeterias, coffee shops, and the campus bookstore

Employees are eligible to use permit-parking facilities. Wage and Salary

Definitions of Employment Status
Exempt: Positions that meet specific tests established by the Fair Labor Standards Act (FLSA) and applicable state law; incumbents are not legally entitled to overtime pay requirements.
Nonexempt: Positions that do not meet FLSA and state exemption tests. Employees are paid overtime for hours worked in excess of 40 per week.
Regular Full-Time: Employees scheduled to work 37.5 hours or more per week.
Regular Part-Time: Employees who are scheduled to work less than 37.5 hours per week but at least 20 hours per week.
Part-Time: Employees who are scheduled to work less than 20 hours per week.
0-Hour: Employees who work as needed and have no regularly scheduled hours.
Temporary: Employees who are hired for a pre-established period of time not to exceed six (6) months, usually during peak workloads or for vacation relief. They may work a full-time or part-time schedule and are ineligible for University benefits.

Salary Increases
Salary rates are typically reviewed once a year; however, frequency of reviews may vary based on circumstances. Brian will explain the timing of performance reviews and salary increases, which typically go into effect July 1.

Paydays
Employees in positions classified as exempt are paid on a monthly basis. Employees in positions classified as nonexempt are paid on a biweekly basis. Those paid monthly are paid on the last working day of the month. Those paid biweekly are paid on the Friday after the end of the pay period.

Payroll Deductions
Various payroll deductions are made each payday to comply with federal and state laws pertaining to taxes and benefits.

Deductions will be made for the following:
• Federal and state income tax withholding
• Social Security (FICA)
• Employee benefits
• Other items designated by the employee

At the end of the calendar year, each employee will be supplied with a Wage and Tax Statement (W-2) form. This statement summarizes income and deductions for the year and may be viewed or printed from Employee Self Service in the University’s payroll system. If questions arise regarding these deductions, please contact the Payroll Department.

Consensual Relationships

When a consensual romantic, dating, or sexual relationship involves one partner holding a position of academic or professional authority over the other partner within the University context, potential issues like favoritism, breaches of trust, and conflicts of interest can arise. This situation may also raise doubts about the nature of the relationship's consent. To address these concerns, the University mandates that partners in such relationships where one partner currently holds a position of authority must promptly report it to their supervisor, department chair, or dean. Arrangements will then be made to either eliminate the authority or mitigate the associated risks. This policy covers situations where a partner's authority extends to matters such as employment, evaluation, recommendation, funding, and more, and applies even when a faculty member and graduate student share the same department or unit.

Undergraduate Students
The University prohibits consensual relationships between undergraduate students and faculty members, and between undergraduate students and employees in administrative or staff roles having broad authority over undergraduate students (e.g., deans and other senior administrators, coaches, academic advisors), regardless of whether the faculty or employee holds a specific position of academic or professional authority with respect to the student.

Noncompliance with Policy
Violation of this policy may result in a range of remedial and disciplinary actions, including but not limited to mandatory training or counseling, reprimand, probation, suspension, loss of privileges, demotion, expulsion, dismissal or termination.

Faculty Rights
Nothing in this policy is intended to abridge the rights of faculty as outlined in the Washington University Policy on Academic Freedom, Responsibility, and Tenure.
