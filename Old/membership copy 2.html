General Lab Expectations

Working Hours

Our lab recognizes the importance of a flexible work environment to accommodate the diverse needs of team members. While we don't enforce strict working hours, we do value regular attendance and effective communication. Staff and Postdoctoral Associates are expected to work 40 hrs/week, graduate students 20 hrs/week, and undergraduates 10 hrs/week. As a member of this lab, you have the flexibility to manage your schedule as long as you fulfill your commitments, meet project deadlines, and maintain open lines of communication with your supervisor and team. We trust that you will prioritize your responsibilities and ensure your work contributes to the overall success of the lab. Together, we can strike a balance between productivity and personal well-being.

Attendance

As a member of our lab, you are expected to attend seminars, weekly lab meetings, 1-on-1 meetings, start and end of semester events, as well as the annual graduate research symposium. These gatherings provide valuable opportunities for knowledge exchange, scientific discussions, networking, and showcasing your research. By attending these events, you contribute to the vibrant and collaborative atmosphere of our lab, while also enhancing your own professional development and building connections within the scientific community.

Communication and Email

Regular team meetings, email updates, and collaborative platforms facilitate seamless information sharing and foster a supportive work environment. We value open dialogue, active listening, and respect for diverse perspectives; and we encourage transparent and timely communication to ensure everyone is aligned on project goals, milestones, and any potential challenges. We appreciate your commitment to maintaining effective and timely communication through email, contributing to a collaborative and productive work environment.

Social Etiquette

In our lab, we not only value effective communication within our team but also recognize the importance of professional conduct in conference environments. We encourage lab members to represent themselves and our research with professionalism and respect when attending conferences or scientific meetings. This includes actively engaging in presentations, asking thoughtful questions, and providing constructive feedback to fellow researchers. We also promote networking and building connections with peers and experts in the field. By upholding high standards of behavior and etiquette in conference settings, we enhance our professional reputation and contribute to the broader scientific community.

Funding

Brian will make every effort to secure grants and funding to support individuals for a couple of years; however, funding availability is competitive and unpredictable. Lab members are encouraged to actively pursue external funding opportunities, and Brian will provide guidance and mentorship throughout the application process. Taking an active role in seeking funding enhances skills and opens doors for future career opportunities.

Publications

As lab members, you will have the opportunity to contribute to the development and writing of manuscripts, ensuring that your intellectual contributions are recognized and acknowledged. Authorship is determined based on the established criteria of substantial intellectual contribution and active participation in the research process. We adhere to ethical standards and guidelines for authorship, promoting fairness, transparency, and accountability. Our lab encourages collaborative and interdisciplinary research, fostering opportunities for co-authorship with colleagues within and outside the lab. By actively engaging in the publication process, we aim to advance scientific knowledge, enhance our professional reputation, and contribute to the scientific community at large.

Career Development and Individual Mentoring Plans

We prioritize supporting postdocs, graduate students, and other lab members in their professional growth. Through mentorship, guidance, and access to relevant resources, we aim to empower individuals to explore their career options, develop necessary skills, and make informed decisions about their future paths. We believe that investing in career development and fostering mentor-mentee relationships contribute to the success and satisfaction of our lab members.

We prioritize individual mentoring plans to support the professional growth and development of each member. These personalized plans are designed to identify and address specific career goals, skill-building opportunities, and areas for improvement. Through regular 1-on-1 meetings, we work closely with our lab members to provide guidance, mentorship, and resources tailored to their unique needs. Our goal is to foster a supportive and collaborative environment that empowers individuals to reach their full potential and achieve their career aspirations.

Graduate Students

As a member of our lab, it is essential that you take ownership of your degree and academic journey. This entails staying proactive and well-informed about program and university deadlines, requirements, and any other pertinent information. By remaining organized and attentive to these responsibilities, you can ensure a smooth and successful progression towards your degree. We are here to support and guide you along the way, but ultimately, the responsibility for staying on top of your academic obligations lies with you. Together, we can create a productive and rewarding experience during your time in our lab.

Postdoctoral Research Associates

As a postdoctoral researcher in our lab, it is crucial that you take charge of your professional development and career advancement. You are responsible for staying informed about relevant opportunities, deadlines, and requirements that are essential for your success. This includes keeping track of funding opportunities, publication deadlines, conference presentations, and any other professional commitments. By actively engaging in these aspects of your career, you can maximize your potential for growth and make significant contributions to your field. While we are here to provide guidance and support, the responsibility for managing your postdoctoral journey lies with you. Together, we can create a stimulating and impactful research environment that fosters your professional growth and success.

Maintaining Health

We understand the significance of maintaining a healthy work-life balance and encourage everyone to prioritize self-care, manage their workload effectively, and set boundaries to ensure a balance between work and personal life. We believe that taking time for oneself, pursuing hobbies, spending quality time with loved ones, and engaging in activities outside of work contribute to overall productivity and satisfaction. By fostering a supportive and flexible environment, we aim to help our lab members achieve a sustainable work-life balance that promotes both personal happiness and professional growth.

Leaving the Lab

When the time comes to leave our lab, we strive to make the transition as smooth as possible. We understand that career paths may diverge, and we support individuals in their decisions to pursue new opportunities. We encourage open communication and appreciate the contributions made during their time with us. Exiting lab members are encouraged to maintain connections and utilize the resources available for networking and future collaborations. Our goal is to foster a supportive environment even beyond their tenure, promoting growth and success in their future endeavors.

Code of Conduct

We value integrity and uphold ethical standards for the responsible use of resources and data. Harassment, discrimination, and any form of misconduct will not be tolerated. We work together as a team, supporting and uplifting one another, and recognizing the contributions of all members. By adhering to this code of conduct, we create a positive and enriching experience for everyone in our lab and university community.
